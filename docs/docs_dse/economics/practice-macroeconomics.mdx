### GDP and GNP Measurement

export const practiceQuestions = [ { question: "Which of the following transactions should be included in
Hong Kong's GDP calculation?", options: [ "A) The sale of a second-hand car to a friend", "B) A
parent staying at home to look after children", "C) A Hong Kong firm producing goods in a factory in

explanation: "GDP measures the total market value of all final goods and services produced within a

GDP." }, { question: "If nominal GDP increases by 8% and the GDP deflator increases by 3%, real GDP

decimal index). Using the approximation: % change in real GDP = % change in nominal GDP - % change

approximately 5%." }, { question: "Which of the following is a limitation of using GDP per capita as

distribution of income or non-market activities", "D) GDP per capita always overstates living
standards in developing countries" ], correct: 2, explanation: "GDP per capita (GDP divided by
population) is a crude measure of average living standards. Its limitations include: it does not

excludes depreciation while GNP includes it" ], correct: 1, explanation: "GDP measures the value of
production within a country's geographical borders, regardless of who owns the factors of
production. GNP (Gross National Product) measures the value of production by a country's residents,

### Inflation and Unemployment

export const practiceQuestions = [ { question: "Demand-pull inflation occurs when:", options: [ "A) Costs
of production increase, shifting aggregate supply leftward", "B) Aggregate demand grows faster than
aggregate supply", "C) Consumers switch demand from one product to another", "D) The government
imposes higher indirect taxes" ], correct: 1, explanation: "Demand-pull inflation is caused by
excess aggregate demand relative to aggregate supply -- 'too much money chasing too few goods'. When
aggregate demand increases faster than the economy's productive capacity, firms raise prices. This
is shown by a rightward shift of the AD curve on the AD-AS diagram. Cost-push inflation, in
contrast, is caused by rising production costs shifting the AS curve leftward." }, { question: "The
Consumer Price Index (CPI) in Hong Kong measures:", options: [ "A) The average change in wholesale
prices of goods", "B) The change in the price level of a representative basket of goods and services
consumed by households", "C) The total value of all goods produced in Hong Kong", "D) The average
wage rate in Hong Kong" ], correct: 1, explanation: "The CPI measures the percentage change over
time in the cost of a fixed basket of goods and services representative of household consumption
patterns. In Hong Kong, the Census and Statistics Department compiles the CPI based on the
expenditure patterns of households. It is the most commonly used indicator of inflation for
measuring changes in the cost of living." }, { question: "Structural unemployment is best described
as:", options: [ "A) Unemployment caused by a general downturn in the business cycle", "B)
Unemployment due to workers moving between jobs", "C) Unemployment caused by a mismatch between
workers' skills and the requirements of available jobs", "D) Unemployment that exists even when the
economy is at full employment" ], correct: 2, explanation: "Structural unemployment arises from a
mismatch between the skills and location of unemployed workers and the requirements of available
jobs. It can be caused by technological change, changes in consumer demand, geographical mobility
issues, or decline of certain industries. It requires retraining or relocation to resolve.
Frictional unemployment is short-term job-to-job transition; cyclical unemployment is due to
economic downturns." }, { question: "Cost-push inflation is most likely to be caused by:", options:
[ "A) An increase in government spending", "B) A rise in wages not matched by productivity gains",
"C) A decrease in interest rates", "D) An increase in consumer confidence" ], correct: 1,
explanation: "Cost-push inflation results from increases in costs of production that shift the
aggregate supply curve leftward. Causes include rising wages (without corresponding productivity
increases), higher raw material prices (e.g., oil price shocks), increased indirect taxes, or a
depreciation of the exchange rate raising import prices. Firms pass on higher costs to consumers in
the form of higher prices." } ];

### Government Policy

export const practiceQuestions = [ { question: "An expansionary fiscal policy involves:", options: [ "A)

decreasing taxation", "C) Reducing the money supply through open market operations", "D) Imposing

policy aims to stimulate aggregate demand and economic growth. It involves increasing government

cutting spending and/or raising taxes to reduce AD and control inflation." }, { question: "When the
central bank raises the base interest rate, which of the following is most likely to occur?",

borrowing more expensive for firms (reducing investment) and households (reducing consumption,
particularly on credit-financed goods like houses and cars). This reduces aggregate demand, helping
to control inflation. Higher interest rates also tend to attract foreign capital inflows, which may
cause the exchange rate to appreciate. This is a contractionary monetary policy." }, { question:
"Which of the following is an example of a supply-side policy?", options: [ "A) Increasing
unemployment benefits to support household income", "B) Providing subsidies for firms to invest in
new technology", "C) Lowering interest rates to stimulate borrowing", "D) Increasing government
spending on infrastructure" ], correct: 1, explanation: "Supply-side policies aim to increase the
productive capacity of the economy by improving the quality and quantity of factors of production.

deregulation, reducing corporate tax rates to incentivise investment, and promoting labour market
flexibility. Unlike demand-side policies, they work by shifting aggregate supply rightward rather
than aggregate demand." }, { question: "The crowding-out effect occurs when:", options: [ "A)

interest rates, which reduce private investment", "C) Government spending replaces all private

crowding-out effect occurs when increased government borrowing to finance deficit spending raises

### International Trade and Money and Banking
