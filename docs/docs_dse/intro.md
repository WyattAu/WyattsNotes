## Exam Structure

The HKDSE is typically taken at the end of Secondary 6 (Year 6). Each subject is assessed through a
combination of public examinations and school-based assessments (SBA). Results are reported on a
scale of **1 to 5\*\*\***, with 5\*\*\* being the highest.

### Key Dates

- **SBA deadlines** — set by individual schools, typically Term 2 of Year 6
- **Public examinations** — April to May each year
- **Results release** — July

### Grade Boundaries

DSE grades are criterion-referenced rather than norm-referenced. The HKEAA sets cut scores based on
expert judgement and statistical analysis. Level 5\*\*\* represents the top ~1% of candidates.

## Study Strategy

1. **Start with the syllabus** — read the HKEAA official curriculum document for each subject to
   understand what is and is not examinable.
2. **Practise past papers** — DSE questions follow consistent patterns. The HKEAA publishes past
   papers with marking schemes — use them.
3. **Manage your time** — the DSE is taken across multiple subjects simultaneously. Create a
   revision timetable that allocates more time to weaker subjects.
4. **Focus on SBA early** — the school-based assessment component can contribute significantly to
   your final grade. Submit your best work.

### Subject-Level Advice

- **ICT:** Practise SQL queries and programming questions hands-on. The SPA (School-based
  Assessment) project requires a complete system — plan your database schema early and document
  everything.
- **Mathematics:** Focus on algebraic manipulation speed and accuracy. The compulsory paper tests a
  broad range of topics — identify your weakest areas and drill them.
- **Extended Mathematics (M1/M2):** M1 emphasises calculus and statistics; M2 emphasises algebra and
  calculus. Choose based on your university course requirements.
