### Scalars and Vectors

export const practiceQuestions = [ { question: "Which of the following quantities is a scalar?", options:
["A) Velocity", "B) Displacement", "C) Speed", "D) Force"], correct: 2, explanation: "Speed has
magnitude only and no direction, making it a scalar. Velocity has both magnitude and direction (a
vector). Displacement is the straight-line distance from start to finish in a given direction. Force
has magnitude and direction." }, { question: "A student walks 3 km east and then 4 km north. What is
the student's displacement?", options: ["A) 7 km", "B) 5 km at 53 degrees east of north", "C) 5 km",
"D) 7 km at 37 degrees east of north"], correct: 1, explanation: "Displacement is the straight-line
distance from start to finish. By Pythagoras: sqrt(3^2 + 4^2) = 5 km. The direction is arctan(3/4) =
36.9 degrees east of north, approximately 37 degrees. Distance walked would be 7 km, but
displacement is only 5 km." }, { question: "Two forces of 5 N and 12 N act at right angles to each
other. What is the magnitude of the resultant force?", options: ["A) 7 N", "B) 13 N", "C) 17 N", "D)
8.5 N"], correct: 1, explanation: "Using the tip-to-tail method or vector addition: R = sqrt(5^2 +
12^2) = sqrt(25 + 144) = sqrt(169) = 13 N. The resultant acts at arctan(5/12) = 22.6 degrees to the
12 N force." }, ];

### Distance, Displacement, Speed and Velocity

### Acceleration and Graphs

export const practiceQuestions = [ { question: "A velocity-time graph shows a straight line passing through
the origin with a positive gradient. This represents:", options: ["A) An object at rest", "B)
Uniform acceleration from rest", "C) Constant velocity", "D) Deceleration"], correct: 1,
explanation: "A straight line through the origin on a v-t graph with positive gradient means
velocity is directly proportional to time (v = at), which is uniform acceleration starting from rest
(u = 0). The gradient equals the acceleration." }, { question: "The area under a velocity-time graph
from t = 0 to t = 5 s represents:", options: ["A) The acceleration of the object", "B) The distance
travelled by the object", "C) The displacement of the object", "D) The average speed of the
object"], correct: 2, explanation: "The area under a velocity-time graph equals the displacement
(not distance). If the velocity becomes negative at any point, the area below the time axis
represents negative displacement. Displacement = integral of v dt." }, { question: "An object moving
with constant deceleration comes to rest in 4 s after travelling 60 m. What was its initial
velocity?", options: ["A) 15 m/s", "B) 30 m/s", "C) 20 m/s", "D) 10 m/s"], correct: 1, explanation:
"Using v = u + at: v = 0, a = -a0, t = 4, so u = 4\*a0. Using s = ut + 0.5at^2: 60 = u(4) +
0.5(-a0)(16). But a0 = u/4, so 60 = 4u - 2u = 2u. Therefore u = 30 m/s." }, ];

### Equations of Motion (SUVAT)

export const practiceQuestions = [ { question: "A ball is thrown vertically upward with initial velocity 15
m/s. How high does it rise? (Take g = 10 m/s^2)", options: ["A) 11.25 m", "B) 15 m", "C) 22.5 m",
"D) 7.5 m"], correct: 0, explanation: "Using v^2 = u^2 + 2as with v = 0 (at maximum height), u = 15,
a = -10. 0 = 225 + 2(-10)s. s = 225/20 = 11.25 m." }, { question: "A car accelerates uniformly from
rest at 2 m/s^2 for 6 seconds. What distance does it travel?", options: ["A) 36 m", "B) 12 m", "C)
24 m", "D) 48 m"], correct: 0, explanation: "Using s = ut + 0.5at^2 with u = 0, a = 2, t = 6. s =
0 + 0.5(2)(36) = 36 m." }, { question: "A stone is dropped from a cliff of height 45 m. How long
does it take to reach the ground? (g = 10 m/s^2, ignore air resistance)", options: ["A) 3 s", "B)
4.5 s", "C) 9 s", "D) 2 s"], correct: 0, explanation: "Using s = ut + 0.5gt^2 with u = 0, s = 45, g
= 10. 45 = 0.5(10)t^2 = 5t^2. t^2 = 9. t = 3 s." }, ];

### Projectile Motion

export const practiceQuestions = [ { question: "In projectile motion (neglecting air resistance), which of
the following statements is correct?", options: [ "A) The horizontal acceleration is g downwards",
"B) The vertical component of velocity is constant", "C) The horizontal component of velocity
remains constant", "D) The acceleration is zero throughout the flight" ], correct: 2, explanation:
"In projectile motion with no air resistance, the horizontal acceleration is zero (no horizontal
force), so the horizontal component of velocity is constant. The vertical acceleration is g
(gravity, approximately 9.81 m/s^2 downwards), so the vertical component changes continuously." }, {
question: "A ball is projected horizontally from a cliff of height 80 m with speed 20 m/s. How far
from the base of the cliff does the ball land? (g = 10 m/s^2)", options: ["A) 40 m", "B) 80 m", "C)
160 m", "D) 20 m"], correct: 1, explanation: "Time to fall: s = 0.5gt^2, 80 = 5t^2, t = 4 s.
Horizontal distance = v_x _ t = 20 _ 4 = 80 m. The vertical and horizontal motions are independent."
}, { question: "For a projectile launched at an angle theta to the horizontal with initial speed v0,
the horizontal range is maximum when:", options: [ "A) theta = 30 degrees", "B) theta = 45 degrees",
"C) theta = 60 degrees", "D) theta = 90 degrees" ], correct: 1, explanation: "The range formula is R
= (v0^2 sin 2*theta) / g. This is maximised when sin(2*theta) = 1, which occurs when 2\*theta = 90
degrees, i.e., theta = 45 degrees. At this angle, complementary angles (e.g., 30 and 60 degrees)
give the same range." }, ];

### Stopping Distance

export const practiceQuestions = [ { question: "A driver's reaction time is 0.8 s. When the driver sees a

25 x 0.8 = 20 m. This is the distance the car travels before the driver begins braking." }, {
question: "Which of the following factors increases the braking distance but NOT the thinking
distance?", options: [ "A) Higher speed", "B) Worn tyres", "C) Alcohol consumption", "D) Wet road

question: "If a car's speed doubles from 20 m/s to 40 m/s, by what factor does its braking distance

braking distance s = u^2/(2a). If speed doubles (u -> 2u), s -> (2u)^2/(2a) = 4u^2/(2a) = 4s.
Braking distance increases by a factor of 4 (quadruples)." }, ];
