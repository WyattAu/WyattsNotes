### Population and Settlement

<PracticeProblem question="A country has a crude birth rate of 35 per 1,000 and a crude death rate of 12 per 1,000. What is the rate of natural increase?" options={['12 per 1,000', '23 per 1,000', '35 per 1,000', '47 per 1,000']} correctAnswer={1} explanation="The rate of natural increase (RNI) is calculated as: Crude Birth Rate minus Crude Death Rate. RNI = 35 - 12 = 23 per 1,000. This means the population is growing by 23 people per 1,000 per year through natural increase alone (excluding migration)." difficulty="easy" />

<PracticeProblem question="A population pyramid with a wide base, narrowing steadily to a small apex, is characteristic of which stage of the Demographic Transition Model?" options={[ 'Stage 1 -- High stationary', 'Stage 2 -- Early expanding', 'Stage 4 -- Low stationary', 'Stage 5 -- Decline', ]} correctAnswer={1} explanation="A wide base (high proportion of young people) with rapid narrowing to a small apex (few elderly) indicates high birth rates and falling death rates, characteristic of Stage 2 of the DTM. In Stage 2, improved healthcare and sanitation reduce death rates (especially infant mortality) while birth rates remain high, leading to rapid population growth and a pyramid with many young dependents." difficulty="easy" />

<PracticeProblem question="Which of the following is the most significant pull factor attracting rural migrants to megacities in developing countries?" options={[ 'Warmer climate in urban areas', 'Greater employment opportunities and higher wages', 'Lower population density in cities', 'More agricultural land available', ]} correctAnswer={1} explanation="The most significant pull factor for rural-to-urban migration is the availability of employment opportunities and higher wages in urban areas. Megacities offer diverse job opportunities in manufacturing, services, and the informal sector, with wages significantly higher than rural agricultural work. This economic incentive is the primary driver of rural-urban migration in developing countries." difficulty="medium" />

### Economic Activities

<PracticeProblem question="According to von Thunen's model, which type of farming would be located closest to the central market?" options={[ 'Extensive cattle ranching', 'Grain farming', 'Dairy farming and vegetable production', 'Commercial forestry', ]} correctAnswer={2} explanation="Von Thunen's model predicts that perishable and high-value products are located closest to the market because they have high transport costs and spoil quickly. Dairy farming and vegetable production are perishable, bulky, and require daily delivery, so farmers locate them nearest to the market to minimise transport costs and losses. Extensive activities like ranching and forestry are placed furthest from the market where land is cheaper." difficulty="medium" />

<PracticeProblem question="A transnational corporation (TNC) decides to build a new factory in a Special Economic Zone (SEZ) in southern China. Which of the following is the most likely reason for this location choice?" options={[ 'Proximity to raw material deposits', 'Government incentives including tax breaks and relaxed regulations', 'Large domestic market for luxury goods', 'Abundant renewable energy sources', ]} correctAnswer={1} explanation="Special Economic Zones (SEZs) offer government incentives such as tax breaks, reduced tariffs, relaxed labour regulations, and streamlined bureaucracy to attract foreign investment. While proximity to raw materials and markets can be factors, the defining feature of SEZs is their favourable government policies. TNCs locate in SEZs primarily to take advantage of these economic incentives, lower operating costs, and export-oriented infrastructure." difficulty="medium" />

<PracticeProblem question="Which of the following best describes the concept of 'agglomeration economies' in industrial location?" options={[ 'The tendency for industries to disperse across rural areas', 'The cost savings and benefits firms gain by locating near each other', 'The decline of manufacturing in developed countries', 'Government restrictions on industrial development near cities', ]} correctAnswer={1} explanation="Agglomeration economies refer to the cost savings and competitive advantages that firms gain by clustering geographically near other firms in related industries. Benefits include shared infrastructure, access to a skilled labour pool, knowledge spillovers, supplier networks, and reduced transport costs. This explains why industries such as technology (Silicon Valley) or finance (London) tend to cluster in specific areas rather than disperse randomly." difficulty="medium" />

<PracticeProblem question="A country's energy mix shifts from 70% coal to 40% coal, 30% natural gas, and 30% renewable energy over 20 years. Which environmental benefit is most directly associated with this shift?" options={[ 'Increased biodiversity in mining regions', 'Reduction in greenhouse gas emissions per unit of energy generated', 'Lower cost of electricity for consumers', 'Increased energy security through diversification', ]} correctAnswer={1} explanation="Shifting from coal (the most carbon-intensive fossil fuel) to natural gas (which produces approximately 50% less CO2 per unit of energy than coal) and renewable energy (which produces minimal or no CO2 during operation) directly reduces greenhouse gas emissions per unit of energy generated. While energy security and potentially biodiversity may improve, the most direct and significant environmental benefit is the reduction in carbon emissions." difficulty="medium" />

<PracticeProblem question="The Green Revolution increased agricultural yields in developing countries primarily through:" options={[ 'Expanding the area of cultivated land', 'Introducing high-yielding crop varieties, chemical fertilisers, and irrigation', 'Converting subsistence farming to organic methods', 'Relocating farming to more fertile regions', ]} correctAnswer={1} explanation="The Green Revolution (1960s-1970s) dramatically increased agricultural yields through the introduction of high-yielding variety (HYV) seeds (especially wheat and rice), chemical fertilisers and pesticides, and expanded irrigation infrastructure. These technological inputs allowed significantly more food to be produced on the same amount of land, though they also raised environmental concerns about soil degradation, water pollution, and dependency on chemical inputs." difficulty="medium" />

### Physical Geography

<PracticeProblem question="At which type of plate boundary is a volcanic island arc most likely to form?" options={[ 'Divergent boundary', 'Continental-continental convergent boundary', 'Oceanic-continental convergent boundary', 'Transform boundary', ]} correctAnswer={2} explanation="Volcanic island arcs form at oceanic-continental convergent boundaries where an oceanic plate subducts beneath a continental plate. As the oceanic plate descends into the mantle, it melts and generates magma that rises through the continental plate, forming a chain of volcanoes parallel to the boundary. Examples include the Andes (South America) and the Cascade Range (North America). Divergent boundaries produce mid-ocean ridges, not island arcs. Continental-continental collisions produce fold mountains (e.g., Himalayas)." difficulty="hard" />

<PracticeProblem question="A river floodplain is formed primarily by which processes?" options={[ 'Only erosion by the river during flood events', 'Deposition of alluvium (sediment) when the river overflows its banks during flood events', 'Chemical weathering of bedrock in the river channel', 'Tectonic uplift along the river valley', ]} correctAnswer={1} explanation="River floodplains are formed by the deposition of alluvium (fine sediment such as silt, clay, and sand) when a river overflows its banks during flood events. As floodwater spreads across the flat land adjacent to the channel, it loses energy and velocity, causing sediment to settle and build up in layers over time. This creates the flat, fertile floodplain characteristic of mature river valleys. While erosion occurs in the channel, it is deposition on the banks that creates the floodplain." difficulty="hard" />
