<PracticeProblem
  question="In $^{1}$H NMR, the chemical shift range for aromatic protons is typically:"
  options={[

    '$\\delta$ 0.5-2.0 ppm (upfield, aliphatic region)',
    '$\\delta$ 3.0-4.5 ppm (region for protons on oxygen-bearing carbons)',
    '$\\delta$ 9.0-10.0 ppm (aldehyde region)',
  ]}
  correctAnswer={0}
  explanation"Aromatic protons appear downfield (6.5-8.0 ppm) due to the diamagnetic ring current of the aromatic $\\pi$ system, which creates a deshielding region above and below the ring plane. Aliphatic protons appear upfield (0.5-2.0 ppm). Aldehyde protons appear furthest downfield (9.0-10.0 ppm). The reference TMS is at 0 ppm."
  difficulty="easy"
/>

<PracticeProblem
  question="The $n+1$ rule in proton NMR predicts that a proton with $n$ equivalent neighbouring protons will show a multiplet with how many peaks?"
  options={[
    '$n+1$ peaks (a triplet for $n=2$, a quartet for $n=3$)',
    '$n$ peaks',
    '$2n+1$ peaks',
    '$n^2$ peaks',
  ]}
  correctAnswer={0}
  explanation"First-order (weakly coupled) spin-spin splitting: a signal splits into $n+1$ peaks where $n$ is the number of equivalent neighbouring protons. The intensity pattern follows Pascal's triangle: doublet (1:1), triplet (1:2:1), quartet (1:3:3:1). The coupling constant $J$ (Hz) is the spacing between peaks and is independent of the external field strength."
  difficulty="easy"
/>

## Retrosynthetic Analysis and Synthesis

<PracticeProblem
  question="What is the first step in a retrosynthetic analysis of benzyl alcohol ($\\text{PhCH}_2\\text{OH}$)?"
  options={[
    'Disconnect the C-OH bond: PhCHO + a suitable hydride source (e.g., NaBH$_4$ or Grignard + formaldehyde)',
    'Disconnect the benzene ring from the side chain',
    'Disconnect the C-C bond in the ring',
    'No disconnection is possible for this simple molecule',
  ]}
  correctAnswer={0}
  explanation"Retrosynthetic analysis of PhCH$_2$OH: disconnect C-OH bond to give PhCHO (benzaldehyde). PhCHO can come from oxidation of PhCH$_3$ (toluene) or from a Friedel-Crafts formylation (Gattermann-Koch). Alternatively, a Grignard reagent PhMgBr + formaldehyde gives the primary alcohol directly. The synthon analysis identifies the electrophilic carbonyl carbon and nucleophilic OH."
  difficulty="medium"
/>
