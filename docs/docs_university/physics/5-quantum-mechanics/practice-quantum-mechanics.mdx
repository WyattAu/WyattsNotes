### Angular Momentum and Quantum Statistics

<PracticeProblem
  question="Two spin-1/2 particles are in the triplet state with S_z = 0 (one of the |1,0> states). If you measure S_z for each particle individually, what is the probability of finding both particles with spin up?"
  options={['0', '1/2', '1/4', '1']}
  correctAnswer={0}
  explanation="The |1,0> triplet state is (|up,down> + |down,up>)/sqrt(2). This is an entangled state -- the particles are perfectly anti-correlated. Measuring S_z for particle 1 gives +hbar/2 or -hbar/2 with equal probability, and particle 2 always gives the opposite.
