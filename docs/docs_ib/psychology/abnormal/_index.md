# Abnormal Psychology

This section covers the HL extension unit on abnormal psychology, examining how psychological disorders
are defined, classified, and explained from biological, cognitive, and sociocultural perspectives.

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: Explaining OCD from Two Perspectives
**Problem:** Explain obsessive-compulsive disorder using biological and cognitive approaches.
**Solution:** **Biological:** OCD is associated with abnormalities in the frontal lobe and basal ganglia, particularly overactivity in the orbitofrontal cortex. Serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) are the primary biomedical treatment, supporting the neurotransmitter hypothesis. **Cognitive:** The cognitive approach suggests that individuals with OCD experience intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and believe they are responsible for preventing harm. Compulsive behaviours are maintained by negative reinforcement (anxiety reduction). Treatment: CBT with exposure and response prevention (ERP).

### Example 2: Evaluating CBT for Depression
**Problem:** Evaluate the effectiveness of CBT as a treatment for depression.
**Solution:** CBT is effective for mild to moderate depression, with research showing comparable outcomes to antidepressant medication in many trials (e.g., Butler et al. meta-analysis). Strengths: no pharmacological side effects; teaches skills for long-term coping; addresses root cognitive patterns. Limitations: requires motivated clients; may be less effective for severe depression; therapist availability varies. Cultural considerations: CBT's emphasis on individual cognitive change may not align with collectivist cultural values.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Describing treatments without linking them to explanations:** Every treatment should be justified by the explanation it is derived from (e.g., SSRIs are justified by the serotonin hypothesis of depression).
- **Ignoring ethical considerations:** Informed consent, right to withdraw, protection from harm, and cultural sensitivity are all relevant when discussing psychological treatments and must be addressed.

## Summary

The abnormal psychology HL extension covers anxiety disorders, depression, and treatment evaluation. Disorders are explained from biological (genetics, neurotransmitters), cognitive (maladaptive schemas, cognitive biases), and sociocultural (social stressors, cultural norms) perspectives. Treatments are evaluated on effectiveness, appropriateness, and ethical grounds.
