## Worked Examples

### Example 1: Explaining the Role of Neurotransmitters
**Problem:** Explain how serotonin influences behaviour, using empirical evidence.
**Solution:** Serotonin is a neurotransmitter associated with mood regulation, sleep, and appetite. Low serotonin levels are linked to depression (supporting the use of SSRIs). Kasamo et al. found that serotonin transporter density was reduced in patients with major depressive disorder. However, the relationship is not straightforward: serotonin also interacts with other neurotransmitters (dopamine, norepinephrine) and varies across brain regions, meaning the simple "serotonin deficiency = depression" model is an oversimplification.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Confusing fMRI and PET:** fMRI measures blood oxygenation (structure-function mapping); PET uses radioactive tracers to track neurotransmitter activity or glucose metabolism. Know the distinction for method questions.
- **Equating neuroplasticity with brain damage recovery:** Neuroplasticity includes both functional recovery after injury and experience-dependent changes (e.g., Maguire's taxi driver study). Both forms should be acknowledged.
