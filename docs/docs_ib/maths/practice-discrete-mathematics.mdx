### Combinatorics

<PracticeProblem question="How many ways can 5 people be arranged in a line?" options={['120', '25', '60', '720']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120." difficulty="easy" />

<PracticeProblem question="How many 3-person committees can be formed from 10 people?" options={['120', '720', '30', '1000']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="This is a combination: C(10,3) = 10!/(3! × 7!) = (10 × 9 × 8)/(3 × 2 × 1) = 120." difficulty="easy" />

<PracticeProblem question="A password consists of 4 distinct digits chosen from {0,1,2,...,9}. How many possible passwords are there?" options={['5040', '10000', '210', '720']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="Permutation of 4 from 10: ¹⁰P₄ = 10 × 9 × 8 × 7 = 5040." difficulty="medium" />

<PracticeProblem question="By the pigeonhole principle, in any group of 13 people, at least how many share the same birth month?" options={['2', '1', '3', '13']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="There are 12 months (pigeonholes) and 13 people (pigeons). <PracticeProblem question="Using inclusion-exclusion, in a class of 40 students: 25 study Maths, 20 study Physics, 10 study both. <PracticeProblem question="How many 4-digit numbers can be formed from {1,2,3,4,5,6} if digits can be repeated?" options={['1296', '360', '720', '15']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="Each of the 4 positions can be filled in 6 ways (with repetition). Total = 6⁴ = 1296." difficulty="hard" />
