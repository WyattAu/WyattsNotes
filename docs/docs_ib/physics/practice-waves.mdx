### Diffraction, Interference and Standing Waves

export const practiceQuestions = [ {question: "For destructive interference to occur between two coherent
sources, the path difference must be:", options: ["A) nλ (where n = 0, 1, 2, ...)", "B) (n + 0.5)λ
(where n = 0, 1, 2, ...)", "C) 2nλ", "D) Zero only"], correct: 1, explanation: "Destructive
interference occurs when the path difference is (n + ½)λ, meaning the waves arrive half a wavelength
out of phase and cancel. Constructive interference occurs at path differences of nλ."}, {question:
"Two coherent sources are 0.50 mm apart. Interference fringes are observed on a screen 2.0 m away.
If the fringe spacing is 2.4 mm, what is the wavelength of the light?", options: ["A) 600 nm", "B)
480 nm", "C) 550 nm", "D) 700 nm"], correct: 0, explanation: "Using the double-slit formula: λ =
ax/D where a = slit spacing, D = screen distance, x = fringe spacing. λ = (0.50 × 10⁻³ × 2.4 × 10⁻³)
/ 2.0 = 1.2 × 10⁻⁶ / 2.0 = 6.0 × 10⁻⁷ m = 600 nm."}, {question: "A standing wave on a string fixed
at both ends has a frequency of 120 Hz for the third harmonic. What is the fundamental frequency?",
options: ["A) 30 Hz", "B) 40 Hz", "C) 60 Hz", "D) 360 Hz"], correct: 1, explanation: "For a standing
wave, the nth harmonic frequency is f_n = n × f₁. The third harmonic (n=3) is 120 Hz, so f₁ = 120/3
= 40 Hz. The fundamental frequency is always the lowest frequency at which a standing wave can
form."}, {question: "Sound waves of frequency 850 Hz are diffracted through a doorway of width 0.90
m. The speed of sound is 340 m s⁻¹. What is the angle of the first diffraction minimum from the
central maximum?", options: ["A) 26.5°", "B) 48.6°", "C) 22.2°", "D) 13.3°"], correct: 0,
explanation: "λ = v/f = 340/850 = 0.400 m. For single slit diffraction: sin θ = λ/a = 0.400/0.90 =
0.444. θ = 26.4° ≈ 26.5°. Diffraction is significant when λ is comparable to or larger than the slit
width."} ];

### Doppler Effect and Polarization

export const practiceQuestions = [ {question: "A police car sounding a siren of frequency 512 Hz moves

"Using the Doppler equation for a moving source: f_observed = f_source × v/(v − v_source) = 512 ×
340/(340 − 30) = 512 × 340/310 = 512 × 1.097 = 561.5 Hz ≈ 560 Hz. The observed frequency is higher
when the source approaches."}, {question: "Only transverse waves can be polarized because:",

higher frequency than longitudinal waves"], correct: 1, explanation: "Polarization restricts the

3... antinodes = n."} ];
