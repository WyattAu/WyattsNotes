### Group 1 and 17 Trends

<PracticeProblem question="Which Group 1 element has the lowest melting point?" options={['Cesium', 'Lithium', 'Sodium', 'Rubidium']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="Group 1 melting points decrease down the group: Li (180 °C) > Na (98 °C) > K (63 °C) > Rb (39 °C) > Cs (28 °C). ### Transition Metal Properties

<PracticeProblem question="What is the electron configuration of Cu²⁺?" options={['[Ar] 3d⁹', '[Ar] 3d⁸ 4s¹', '[Ar] 3d¹⁰', '[Ar] 4s² 3d⁷']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="Copper has the anomalous ground state configuration [Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s¹ because a fully-filled 3d subshell provides extra stability. A common error is writing [Ar]  3d⁸ 4s¹, but transition metal ions always lose their s-electrons before d-electrons." difficulty="easy" />

<PracticeProblem question="Which property is NOT characteristic of transition metals?" options={[ 'They all form colourless compounds in at least one common oxidation state.', 'They exhibit variable oxidation states.', 'They form coloured compounds.', 'They and their compounds often show catalytic activity.', ]} correctAnswer={0} explanation="Transition metals are defined by their properties: variable oxidation states (due to similar energies of 4s and 3d electrons), coloured compounds (from d-d electron transitions), and catalytic activity (providing alternative reaction pathways with lower activation energy via variable oxidation states).
