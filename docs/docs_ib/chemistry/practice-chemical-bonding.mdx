### Bonding Types

<PracticeProblem question="Which compound has the highest lattice energy?" options={['MgO', 'NaCl', 'CaF₂', 'LiF']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="Lattice energy depends on the charges of the ions and the distance between them (U ∝ |q₁q₂|/r). MgO has Mg²⁺ and O²⁻ ions (charges of +2 and -2), giving |q₁q₂| = 4. NaCl has Na⁺ and Cl⁻ (|q₁q₂| = 1). CaF₂ has Ca²⁺ and F⁻ (|q₁q₂| = 2 per F⁻). LiF has Li⁺ and F⁻ (|q₁q₂| = 1). <PracticeProblem question="Which molecule is non-polar despite having polar covalent bonds?" options={['CCl₄', 'HCl', 'CH₃Cl', 'NH₃']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="CCl₄ has a tetrahedral geometry with four identical C-Cl bonds. HCl is diatomic and polar. CH₃Cl has three C-H and one C-Cl bond; the dipoles do not cancel. ### Molecular Geometry and Intermolecular Forces

<PracticeProblem question="What is the molecular geometry of XeF₄ according to VSEPR theory?" options={['Square planar', 'Tetrahedral', 'See-saw', 'Square pyramidal']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="XeF₄ has 8 + 4(7) = 36 valence electrons, or 18 electron pairs. The Xe atom has 4 bonding pairs and 2 lone pairs. The electron domain geometry is octahedral (6 domains). The two lone pairs position themselves opposite each other (trans) to minimise repulsion, resulting in a square planar molecular geometry. This is analogous to the arrangement in molecules like ICl₄⁻." difficulty="medium" />

<PracticeProblem question="Which statement correctly describes the intermolecular forces present in liquid CH₃OH (methanol)?" options={[ 'Hydrogen bonding, dipole-dipole forces, and London dispersion forces', 'Only hydrogen bonding', 'Only dipole-dipole forces and London dispersion forces', 'Covalent bonding and London dispersion forces', ]} correctAnswer={0} explanation="CH₃OH (methanol) has an O-H bond, which allows hydrogen bonding (the strongest intermolecular force present). The molecule is also polar (C-O and O-H bond dipoles do not cancel in the tetrahedral-like arrangement), so dipole-dipole forces are present. London dispersion forces exist between all molecules, regardless of polarity. Therefore, all three types of intermolecular forces are present, with hydrogen bonding being the dominant one. Covalent bonding is an intramolecular force, not intermolecular." difficulty="easy" />

<PracticeProblem question="What is the hybridisation of the central atom in SF₆?" options={['sp³d²', 'sp³d', 'sp³', 'd²sp³']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="SF₆ has 6 bonding pairs around the central S atom and no lone pairs. The steric number is 6. To accommodate 6 bonding domains, the S atom undergoes sp³d² hybridisation, using one s, three p, and two d orbitals to form six equivalent hybrid orbitals arranged in an octahedral geometry. The IB data booklet notation uses sp³d² (not d²sp³, which implies a different orbital ordering sometimes used in transition metal complexes)." difficulty="hard" />
