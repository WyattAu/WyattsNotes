### Balancing Equations and Limiting Reactants

<PracticeProblem question="In the reaction: N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) → 2NH₃(g), if 2.00 mol of N₂ and 7.00 mol of H₂ are allowed to react, which is the limiting reactant?" options={['H₂', 'N₂', 'Neither; they are in stoichiometric ratio.', 'NH₃']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="The stoichiometric ratio of H₂:N₂ is 3:1. Alternatively: from 7.00 mol H₂, the required N₂ = 7.00/3 = 2.33 mol, but only 2.00 mol N₂ is available. Either approach confirms H₂ is limiting. Excess N₂ remaining = 2.00 - 7.00/3 = 2.00 - 2.33 = -0.33, confirming H₂ is used up first." difficulty="easy" />
