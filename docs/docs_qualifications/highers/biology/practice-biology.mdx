They can develop into any of the over 200 cell types in the human body as well as extra-embryonic tissues such as the placenta and umbilical cord. They exist only in the earliest stages of development (from the zygote to approximately the 4-8 cell morula stage). By the blastocyst stage, the inner cell mass contains pluripotent stem cells which can form all body cell types but not extra-embryonic tissues. ### Genetics: DNA Replication, Protein Synthesis, and Mutations

The original codon CAG codes for glutamine. The mutated codon TAG is a stop codon, which signals the ribosome to terminate translation prematurely. The resulting protein is likely to be truncated (shorter than normal) and may be non-functional or degraded by cellular quality control mechanisms. This contrasts with missense mutations (which change one amino acid to another), silent mutations (which do not change the amino acid), and frameshift mutations (caused by insertions or deletions that shift the reading frame)." difficulty="hard" />
