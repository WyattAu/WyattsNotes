### Waves

<PracticeProblem
  question="Light travelling in air (refractive index 1.0) enters glass (refractive index 1.5) at an angle of incidence of 30 degrees. What is the angle of refraction in the glass?"
  options={['19.5 degrees', '45 degrees', '20 degrees', '15 degrees']}
  correctAnswer={0}
  explanation="Using Snell's law: n1 sin(theta1) = n2 sin(theta2). Substituting: 1.0 x sin(30) = 1.5 x sin(theta2). Since sin(30) = 0.5: 0.5 = 1.5 x sin(theta2), so sin(theta2) = 0.5/1.5 = 0.333. Therefore theta2 = arcsin(0.333) = 19.5 degrees. The light bends towards the normal when entering a denser medium (higher refractive index), which is why the angle of refraction is smaller than the angle of incidence."

### Electricity

<PracticeProblem
  question="A circuit has a 12 V battery connected to two 6 ohm resistors in parallel. What is the total current drawn from the battery?"
  options={['4 A', '2 A', '1 A', '8 A']}
  correctAnswer={0}
  explanation="For resistors in parallel, the combined resistance R is given by 1/R = 1/R1 + 1/R2 = 1/6 + 1/6 = 2/6 = 1/3, so R = 3 ohms. Using Ohm's law (V = IR), the total current I = V/R = 12/3 = 4 A. Note that the combined resistance of two equal resistors in parallel is always half the individual resistance: 6/2 = 3 ohms. The total current splits equally through each branch: 2 A through each 6 ohm resistor."
  difficulty="medium"
/>

### Heat

  options={['504,000 J', '336,000 J', '168,000 J', '84,000 J']}
  correctAnswer={0}
  explanation="Using the equation Q = mc(delta T), where Q = energy, m = mass = 2 kg, c = specific heat capacity = 4,200 J/(kg degrees C), and delta T = temperature change = 80 - 20 = 60 degrees C. Therefore Q = 2 x 4,200 x 60 = 504,000 J (or 504 kJ). This equation applies to heating a substance without a change of state.
