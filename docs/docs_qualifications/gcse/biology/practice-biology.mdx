### Cell Biology

<PracticeProblem question="Which organelle is responsible for protein synthesis in a cell?" options={['Ribosome', 'Mitochondria', 'Chloroplast', 'Cell membrane']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="Ribosomes are the site of protein synthesis. They read messenger RNA (mRNA) and assemble amino acids into polypeptide chains. Mitochondria produce ATP through respiration, chloroplasts carry out photosynthesis, and the cell membrane controls what enters and leaves the cell." difficulty="easy" />

<PracticeProblem question="During which stage of mitosis do chromosomes line up along the equator of the cell?" options={['Metaphase', 'Prophase', 'Anaphase', 'Telophase']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="During metaphase, spindle fibres pull the chromosomes so they line up along the middle (equator) of the cell. In prophase, chromosomes condense and the nuclear envelope breaks down. In anaphase, sister chromatids are pulled to opposite poles. In telophase, two new nuclei form." difficulty="medium" />

The shorter the wavelength of the radiation used, the higher the resolution. Electrons have a wavelength about 100,000 times shorter than visible light, so electron microscopes can resolve structures down to 0.2 nm compared to around 200 nm for light microscopes." difficulty="hard" />

### Organisation

<PracticeProblem question="In which organ of the digestive system does most of the absorption of nutrients into the bloodstream occur?" options={['Small intestine', 'Stomach', 'Large intestine', 'Liver']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="The small intestine is the main site of nutrient absorption. It has a very large surface area due to villi and microvilli, which increase the rate of absorption. Digested molecules such as glucose and amino acids pass into the blood capillaries of each villus. The stomach digests protein and the large intestine absorbs water." difficulty="easy" />

<PracticeProblem question="Which blood vessel carries oxygenated blood from the lungs back to the heart?" options={['Pulmonary vein', 'Pulmonary artery', 'Aorta', 'Vena cava']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="The pulmonary vein carries oxygenated blood from the lungs to the left atrium of the heart. This is unusual for a vein because most veins carry deoxygenated blood. The pulmonary artery carries deoxygenated blood from the heart to the lungs, the aorta carries oxygenated blood from the heart to the body, and the vena cava carries deoxygenated blood from the body to the heart." difficulty="medium" />

### Bioenergetics

<PracticeProblem question="What is the word equation for photosynthesis?" options={[ 'Carbon dioxide + water -> glucose + oxygen', 'Glucose + oxygen -> carbon dioxide + water', 'Carbon dioxide + oxygen -> glucose + water', 'Glucose + water -> carbon dioxide + oxygen', ]} correctAnswer={0} explanation="Photosynthesis uses carbon dioxide and water, in the presence of light energy absorbed by chlorophyll, to produce glucose and oxygen. The second option describes respiration, not photosynthesis. Chlorophyll is the green pigment found in chloroplasts that absorbs light energy for this process." difficulty="easy" />

<PracticeProblem question="A student calculates the rate of photosynthesis at different light intensities. The rate increases at first but then levels off. Why does the rate stop increasing?" options={[ 'Another factor such as carbon dioxide concentration or temperature has become the limiting factor', 'The plant has run out of chlorophyll', 'Light intensity has reached its maximum possible value', 'The plant begins to respire faster than it photosynthesises', ]} correctAnswer={0} explanation="At low light intensity, light is the limiting factor. As light intensity increases, the rate of photosynthesis rises. However, at higher light intensities, another factor (such as carbon dioxide concentration or temperature) becomes the limiting factor, so the rate plateaus. This is because the enzymes involved in the light-independent reaction (Calvin cycle) are working at their maximum rate." difficulty="hard" />

### Inheritance

<PracticeProblem question="In DNA, which nitrogenous base pairs with adenine?" options={['Thymine', 'Cytosine', 'Guanine', 'Uracil']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="In DNA, adenine (A) always pairs with thymine (T), and cytosine (C) always pairs with guanine (G). These are called complementary base pairs. In RNA, uracil replaces thymine and pairs with adenine. The base pairing rules ensure that DNA can be copied accurately during replication." difficulty="easy" />

<PracticeProblem question="A characteristic controlled by a single gene with two alleles, where the heterozygous phenotype is intermediate between the two homozygous phenotypes, is described as:" options={['Codominant', 'Dominant', 'Recessive', 'Polygenic']} correctAnswer={0} explanation="In codominance, both alleles are expressed equally in the phenotype of the heterozygote, producing an intermediate or blended appearance. An example is red and white flower alleles producing pink flowers. In dominant/recessive inheritance, only the dominant allele is expressed. Polygenic characteristics are controlled by many genes acting together, such as height." difficulty="medium" />
