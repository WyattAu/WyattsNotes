<PracticeProblem
  question="According to the Toulmin model of argumentation, which element connects the evidence (data) to the claim?"
  options={['Backing', 'Qualifier', 'Warrant', 'Rebuttal']}
  correctAnswer={2}
  explanation="In the Toulmin model, the warrant is the logical bridge (often implicit) that connects the evidence (data) to the claim. It explains why the data supports the claim. For example, if the data is 'This vaccine has a 95% efficacy rate in clinical trials' and the claim is 'You should get this vaccine,' the warrant might be 'Vaccines with high efficacy rates in trials effectively prevent disease in the general population.' Backing supports the warrant, qualifiers limit the claim, and rebuttals address counterarguments."
  difficulty="medium"
/>
